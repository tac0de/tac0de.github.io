The city was completed at dawn. When the final pillar of the temple sank into place, the black metal rang beneath the earth, and the sound moved through the jawbones of sleeping citizens, loosening old names.

They said Krontium was a metal drawn from the bones of a god. No one remembered the god's name. It vanished on the first day of smelting, and the kingdom recorded the loss as a blessing.

If a city could be made never to fall, one name was a modest price.

Tax assessor Ian opened the first ledger at the north gate square. It was not paper. It was a thin plate of hammered Krontium, and its surface had not yet learned a single letter. He smiled at the citizens and said:

Memory is fairer than blood.

When he finished speaking, a child in the crowd stopped crying. The child still held his mother's hand, but he no longer remembered whom he had been waiting for. A small sentence rose across the ledger.

First levy. The smell of waiting.
