The king was already dead. His heart had stopped the night before the coronation, and the physicians confirmed it three times. The royal house rang bells instead of preparing a funeral. The Krontium crown had not yet touched his head.

The cathedral ceiling rose like the ribs of the city. Black metal swallowed the candles and returned them as gold. When the priests lifted the crown, the people gathered in the square bit the tips of their tongues at once. It was the pain of contract.

The crown touched the king's brow, and the corpse drew breath.

The crowd should have cheered. Instead the whole square fell briefly silent. Everyone had forgotten the same thing. It was not a first love's face, not a birth village, not the terror of war. It was something smaller and more fatal.

No one remembered the expression the king had worn before he died.

Ian inscribed the second entry in the ledger.

Second levy. The face of death.
